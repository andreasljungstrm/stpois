Dear Editors of the Stata Journal,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "stpois: Fast Poisson
event-history regression with high-dimensional fixed effects", for
publication in the Stata Journal.

The article introduces the community-contributed command stpois, which
fits Poisson event-history models directly on stset data through three
estimation paths that all return the exact maximum-likelihood
estimates: a standard path wrapping poisson, a high-dimensional
fixed-effects path (iteratively reweighted alternating projections in
Mata, with no external dependencies), and a cell-accelerated fast path
that retains continuous covariates. Coefficients, log likelihoods, and
OIM, robust, and cluster-robust standard errors are numerically
identical to the corresponding poisson model on every path, which the
enclosed test suite verifies to 1e-6.

The submission zip contains the manuscript PDF, the ado-files, the
help file, the test suite, the do-file that regenerates every log
shown in the article (all examples use webuse stan3 or seeded
simulated data), the benchmark scripts with their CSV output backing
the timing tables, and readme.txt.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and
reports no conflicts of interest.
